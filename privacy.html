Privacy Policy
Intent

Last updated: March 2026  ·  Effective immediately

Overview

Intent is designed with your privacy as a first principle. The app does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data with the developer or any third party.

Simple version: everything stays on your device. We have no servers, no accounts, no tracking. We literally cannot see your data.

Data Storage

All data you create within Intent — including journal entries, shoot notes, images, and audio recordings — is stored locally on your device in standard iOS system directories. No data is sent to external servers or third parties.

iCloud Backup

If you have iCloud Backup enabled on your device, your Intent data will be included in your standard iOS device backup. This backup is managed entirely by Apple and governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

The developer has no access to iCloud backups and receives no data through this process.

Third-Party Services

Intent contains no third-party SDKs, analytics frameworks, advertising libraries, or tracking tools of any kind.

No analytics No advertising No crash SDKs No social logins No network calls

Crash Reporting

If you have opted into sharing diagnostics with app developers through your iOS Settings, Apple may provide the developer with anonymized crash logs. These contain no personally identifiable information — only technical stack traces used to fix bugs.

You can manage this under Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements.

Data Responsibility

Because all data is stored locally, you are responsible for maintaining backups of your content. We recommend keeping iCloud Backup enabled to protect against device loss or failure. The developer is not liable for data loss resulting from device failure, accidental deletion, iOS updates, or app removal.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated in a future app version, users will be notified via the App Store update release notes. The effective date above will reflect the most recent revision.
