Photography is
deliberate
observation.

Before the shutter is pressed, a photographer has already made a series of decisions — why this scene, why this light, why this moment. That mental act is intent.

The photo alone rarely tells the whole story. Weather, environment, and personal observations often determine why an image succeeded or failed. Intent exists to capture that surrounding context.

It's designed to behave like a field notebook or instrument — something you reach for in the middle of a shoot without thinking, then return to when you're back at your desk trying to understand what you made.
